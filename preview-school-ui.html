AI Learning Tips

1

Use phonics-based reading programs with multi-sensory techniques — students with dyslexia respond well to seeing, hearing, and touching letters simultaneously.

2

Break mathematical problems into smaller visual steps using color-coded manipulatives. Graph paper helps maintain alignment for dyscalculia students.

3

Allow extra time for written assignments and consider oral assessments as alternatives for students with dysgraphia.

4

Use text-to-speech tools during reading so students can focus on comprehension rather than decoding words.

5

Celebrate small wins daily — students with LD often have low confidence. Positive reinforcement builds resilience over time.
